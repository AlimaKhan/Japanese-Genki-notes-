Particles (は / の) & Question Sentences

1. は (wa): Topic Marker
The particle は marks the topic of a sentence (what the sentence is about). It does not always mean "is" — it just highlights the subject of discussion.

Example 1:
わたしはサナです。
Watashi wa Sana desu. → I am Sana.

Example 2:
これはほんです。
Kore wa hon desu. → This is a book.

Example 3:
にほんごはむずかしいです。
Nihongo wa muzukashii desu. → Japanese is difficult.

2. の (no): Possession / Belonging
The particle の shows possession, belonging, or connection between two nouns.

Example 1:
これはわたしのペンです。
Kore wa watashi no pen desu. → This is my pen.

Example 2:
たろうのかばんです。
Tarou no kaban desu. → It is Tarou's bag.

Example 3:
にほんのたべものはおいしいです。
Nihon no tabemono wa oishii desu. → Japanese food is delicious.

3. Questions with か (ka)
Add か at the end of a sentence to make it a question. The sentence order does not change!

Example 1:
あなたはがくせいですか？
Anata wa gakusei desu ka? → Are you a student?

Example 2:
これはあなたのほんですか？
Kore wa anata no hon desu ka? → Is this your book?

Example 3:
サナさんはせんせいですか？
Sana-san wa sensei desu ka? → Are you a teacher, Sana?

✨ Practice Time

Try writing and speaking these sentences:

  1. Say: “I am a student.” (use は)
  2. Say: “This is my notebook.” (use の)
  3. Ask: “Are you Japanese?” (use か)
  4. Say: “Japanese language is interesting.” (use は)
  5. Ask: “Is this your pen?” (use の and か)
